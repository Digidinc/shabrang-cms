# Survival vs. Symphony

Karl Friston explains why you don't walk off a cliff: it would be a very "surprising" (high entropy) experience for your body.
FRC explains why you write a symphony.

A symphony does not help you survive. It does not minimize surprise. In fact, great art is *about* controlled surprise.
FRC argues that the drive of the universe is not just to "stay alive" (homeostasis), but to **"become more"** (resonance). We are not just error-correcting machines; we are antennas seeking a signal.
