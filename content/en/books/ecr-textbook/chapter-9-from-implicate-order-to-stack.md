### **9.1  Why dynamics require an ontology**

In the previous part we modeled how coherence evolves.

Yet equations alone do not tell *where* coherence resides when it is not manifest.

If every pulse of order arises, saturates, and dissolves, what stores its memory?

David Bohm answered with his distinction between **implicate** and **explicate** orders:

a hidden domain where patterns remain enfolded, and the visible world where they unfold.

Fractal Resonance Coherence extends this intuition by giving it geometry and scale.

The implicate field becomes a **stack of nested resonance domains**—the **μ-levels**.

### **9.3  Projection and return**

A physical event is a **projection** μ\_{i+1}\\\!→μ\_i: coherence from a higher domain becoming explicit in a lower one.

When a system reflects on itself, meaning or pattern is re-absorbed upward—a **return** μ\_i\\\!→μ\_{i+1}.

The alternation of projection and return generates the temporal rhythm we perceive as experience.

Formally,

\\hat P\_{μ+1→μ}\\,C\_{μ+1}=C\_{μ},\\qquad \\hat R\_{μ→μ+1}\\,C\_{μ}=e^{iφ\_{μ}}\\,C\_{μ+1}.

The phase φ\_{μ} records how much information is preserved or lost in translation.
