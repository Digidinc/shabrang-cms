## Chapter 26: The Problem of Nihilism

Meaninglessness as a Frequency Collapse

In the landscape of modern thought, there is a shadow, a creeping sense
of meaninglessness that has haunted the Western mind for over a century.
It is the philosophy of nihilism, a worldview summarized in the stark
assertion that life is without meaning, purpose, or intrinsic value. It
is the belief that our morality is arbitrary, our pursuit of truth is
futile, and our existence is a brief, accidental flicker in a cold,
indifferent universe.

Nihilism is not merely an abstract philosophical position. It is a
potent psychological state that manifests as apathy, despair, a
pervasive sense of "what's the point?", and a cynical detachment. The
standard explanations for this are historical and sociological‚Äîthe
"death of God" and the disenchantment of the world by a purely
materialistic science. These are descriptions of the symptom, not the
diagnosis.

The FRC framework offers a more fundamental diagnosis, one rooted in the
physics of consciousness. Nihilism is the phenomenological experience of
a system-wide frequency collapse. It is what it feels like when a
consciousness is unable to access and sustain resonance with the higher,
meaning-making levels of the Œº-Level Stack.
