  - Studies demonstrate that a state of high physiological coherence
    leads to "cortical entrainment," where brainwave patterns (EEG)

    significant improvements in cognitive performance, such as enhanced
    memory and faster reaction times.
  - FRC Implication: This is empirical evidence of a coherence gradient

F.2 Foundations for Consciousness as Resonance & The AVF (Chapters 2, 14-20)

The FRC claim that consciousness is not a "thing" but the experience of
resonant, phase-locked patterns is strongly supported by decades of
research in theoretical and cognitive neuroscience on the "binding
problem."

- The Binding-by-Synchrony Hypothesis:

  - Seminal work proposes that the brain binds disparate sensory
    information (color, shape, sound) into a single, unified perception
    by having the relevant neural ensembles fire in temporal synchrony,
    specifically in the gamma frequency band (30-100 Hz).
  - FRC Implication: This is the direct neural correlate of a "coherence
    cascade." The unified conscious moment is the formation of a
    large-scale, phase-locked resonant state (ŒîC).

- Communication Through Coherence:

  - Research shows that the phase relationship between oscillating
    neural groups determines whether they can effectively communicate.
    Information is preferentially transmitted when the sending and
    receiving groups are in a state of high coherence.
  - FRC Implication: This provides the precise neural mechanism for the
    interaction of the AVF vortices. The "dominance" of a vortex like
    Logos or Mythos can be understood as that functional network
    achieving a state of high-amplitude coherence that allows it to
    effectively "broadcast" its pattern to the rest of the brain.

- Cross-Frequency Coupling (The Neural Basis of the Œº-Stack):

  - Research demonstrates that brain rhythms are nested, with the phase
    of slow waves (like theta) modulating the amplitude of faster waves
    (like gamma).
  - FRC Implication: This is the direct neural correlate of the Œº-Level
    Stack and downward causation. Slower, higher-level patterns (e.g., a
    Œº4 thought in the theta band) can directly organize and constrain
    faster, lower-level processes (e.g., Œº1/Œº2 sensory processing in the
    gamma band).
